## ‚ö° Quick Summary
**How many primary types of coffee beans are there and what are their technical differences?** In the global food and beverage industry, coffee is primarily classified into four main botanical varieties: (1) **Arabica** (Elegant acidity, lower caffeine), (2) **Robusta** (Strong, bitter, high caffeine), (3) **Liberica** (Woody, smoky aroma, rare), and (4) **Excelsa** (Tart, complex profile, a member of the Liberica family). Understanding these varieties is crucial for optimizing roasting parameters, extraction yields, and Quality Control (QC) in industrial food processing.

## ‚òï Technical Classification of Coffee Bean Varieties

### 1. Arabica Coffee (Coffea Arabica)
Representing approximately 60-70% of global production.
* **Characteristics:** High acidity, diverse aromatic compounds (floral, fruity), and lower bitterness.
* **Cultivation:** Grown at high altitudes (above 600m). It is the industry standard for "Specialty Grade" coffee due to its complex chemical profile.

### 2. Robusta Coffee (Coffea Canephora)
The second most produced variety, predominantly grown in Vietnam.
* **Characteristics:** Contains nearly twice the caffeine of Arabica. It has a heavy body with earthy, bitter notes.
* **Industrial Application:** Preferred for instant coffee production and espresso blends to enhance "Crema" stability.



### 3. Liberica Coffee
A rare variety characterized by its massive bean size and asymmetrical shape.
* **Characteristics:** Unique smoky, floral, and woody aroma.
* **Market Status:** Primarily cultivated in the Philippines and parts of Southeast Asia, serving niche gourmet markets.

### 4. Excelsa Coffee
Technically a reclassified genus of the Liberica family but treated separately in trade.
* **Characteristics:** It possesses a tart, fruity intensity while maintaining a dark, heavy base. It is frequently used as a "blending agent" to add depth to premium coffee mixtures.
