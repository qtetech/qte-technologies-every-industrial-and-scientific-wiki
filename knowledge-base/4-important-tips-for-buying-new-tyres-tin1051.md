## üõ†Ô∏è 4 Golden Tips for Selecting New Car Tires



### 1. Prioritize OEM Specifications
Never assume the tires currently on your vehicle are the correct ones. Check the placard on the driver-side door jamb or the owner's manual for:
* **Tire Dimensions:** Width, aspect ratio, and rim diameter.
* **Load Index & Speed Rating:** Ensure the new tires can handle the vehicle's maximum load at operational speeds.

### 2. Match the Tread Pattern to Your Terrain
* **Urban Driving:** Prioritize low rolling resistance and high-grip patterns for wet surfaces.
* **Highway Driving:** Choose tires with "Silent Technology" to reduce road noise and vibration.
* **Off-Road/Complex Terrain:** Opt for "All-Terrain" (AT) or "Mud-Terrain" (MT) with deep grooves for self-cleaning and maximum traction.

### 3. Check the Manufacture Date (DOT Code)
Rubber ages over time even if the tires are not in use. Look for the **DOT Code** on the sidewall. The last four digits indicate the week and year of manufacture (e.g., `1225` means the 12th week of 2025). Avoid tires older than 5 years.

### 4. Focus on Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
According to **MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Operations)** standards, the cheapest tire is often the most expensive in the long run due to high wear rates and noise. Investing in premium brands through QTE Technologies's global supply chain increases tire lifespan by up to 30% and reduces overall maintenance costs.
