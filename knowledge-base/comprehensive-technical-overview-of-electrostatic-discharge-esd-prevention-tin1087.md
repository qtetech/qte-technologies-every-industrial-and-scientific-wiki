## ðŸ” What is ESD (Electrostatic Discharge)?
**Electrostatic Discharge (ESD)** is the sudden flow of electricity between two electrically charged objects caused by contact, an electrical short, or dielectric breakdown. In the electronics industry, even a tiny ESD event (invisible to the human eye) can destroy sensitive components like microchips and sensors.

## ðŸ›  Essential ESD Prevention Strategies

To maintain a safe production environment, QTE Technologies recommends a multi-layered approach to ESD control:

### 1. Personal Grounding Equipment
* **Wrist Straps:** Connect the operator directly to the ground to dissipate static.
* **ESD Footwear & Clothing:** Made from conductive or dissipative materials to prevent charge buildup.

### 2. ESD Safe Workstations
* **ESD Mats:** Placed on tabletops and floors to provide a controlled path to ground.
* **Ionizers:** Used to neutralize static charges on non-conductive surfaces (insulators) where grounding is ineffective.

### 3. Packaging and Storage
* **Static Shielding Bags:** Protect sensitive components during transport.
* **Conductive Bins & Containers:** Ensure all materials within the ESD Protected Area (EPA) are at the same potential.

## ðŸ“ Standards & Compliance
Professional ESD programs should adhere to international standards such as:
* **ANSI/ESD S20.20:** The gold standard for developing an ESD control program.
* **IEC 61340-5-1:** Protection of electronic devices from electrostatic phenomena.
