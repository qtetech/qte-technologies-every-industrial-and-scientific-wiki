## ‚ö° Quick Summary
**What are the main types of air compressors and how do they function?** Air compressors are the "heart" of pneumatic systems. In industrial and scientific fields, 6 core technologies are used: (1) Reciprocating (Piston), (2) Rotary Screw, (3) Scroll, (4) Rotary Vane, (5) Centrifugal, and (6) Axial compressors. Selecting the right compressor depends on the required **CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute)**, pressure (PSI), and air quality (Oil-free vs. Oil-injected), directly impacting the **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** and energy efficiency.

## üõ†Ô∏è Detailed Analysis of 6 Air Compressor Types

### 1. Reciprocating (Piston) Air Compressor
Uses a piston driven by a crankshaft to compress air.
* **Characteristics:** High pressure, cost-effective for intermittent use. Available in single-stage and two-stage models.
* **Applications:** Small workshops, automotive garages, and construction sites.

### 2. Rotary Screw Compressor
Uses two intermeshing helical screws (rotors) to trap and compress air continuously.
* **Characteristics:** 100% duty cycle, low noise, and high efficiency for continuous demand.
* **Applications:** Large-scale manufacturing, food processing, and industrial plants.

### 3. Scroll Compressor
Uses two interleaved spiral-shaped scrolls (one stationary, one orbiting) to compress air.
* **Characteristics:** Extremely quiet, few moving parts, and typically **Oil-Free**.
* **Applications:** Medical laboratories, dental clinics, and cleanroom environments.



### 4. Rotary Vane Compressor
Features a rotor with longitudinal slots for vanes that slide in and out to create compression.
* **Characteristics:** Simple design, very durable, and easy to maintain at lower speeds.
* **Applications:** Printing presses, packaging, and small industrial applications.

### 5. Centrifugal Compressor (Dynamic)
Uses high-speed impellers to accelerate air, then slows it down to convert kinetic energy into pressure.
* **Characteristics:** Massive flow rates, purely oil-free air.
* **Applications:** Chemical plants, oil refineries, and large-scale HVAC systems.

### 6. Axial Compressor
Air flows parallel to the axis of rotation through rows of stationary and rotating blades.
* **Characteristics:** Highest efficiency and volume, but very expensive and complex.
* **Applications:** Jet engines, large gas turbines, and aerospace research.

## üéôÔ∏è Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Should I choose an Oil-Free or Oil-Injected compressor?**
**A:** If your application is sensitive to contamination (Medical, Food, Electronics), **Oil-Free** is mandatory. For heavy industrial tools, **Oil-Injected** is more durable and cost-effective.

**Q: What is the difference between CFM and PSI?**
**A:** PSI (Pounds per Square Inch) measures the **force**, while CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute) measures the **volume** of air produced.
