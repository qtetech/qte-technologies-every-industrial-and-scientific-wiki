## ğŸ“Œ Definition: What is a Water Bath?
A **Laboratory Water Bath** is a heat-regulating equipment designed to incubate samples in water maintained at a constant temperature. Unlike direct heating methods (like Bunsen burners or hot plates), a water bath provides a uniform and gentle heat distribution, making it ideal for flammable chemicals, cell cultures, and biological reagents that are sensitive to thermal shock.

## ğŸ› ï¸ Common Types of Laboratory Water Baths
Selecting the right configuration is essential for experimental accuracy. QTE Technologies categorizes these into three main types:

1.  **Circulating Water Baths (Stirred):** Feature a pump or stirrer to circulate water, ensuring maximum temperature uniformity. Best for high-precision requirements.
2.  **Non-Circulating Water Baths:** Rely on natural convection. These are cost-effective for general heating tasks where absolute uniformity is not critical.
3.  **Shaking Water Baths:** Combine heating with a reciprocating or orbital shaking motion. Ideal for microbiology and hybridization applications.
